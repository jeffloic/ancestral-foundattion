Broken Cultures Festival

Uniting Afro-Diaspora and African royalty to celebrate cultural heritage and promote peace through cultural diplomacy.

About the Festival

The Broken Cultures Festival is designed to unite Afro-Diaspora and African royalty, celebrating multicultural heritage as a mediation tool to foster global unification and peace. Through educational workshops, music, cultural performances, and global participation, this festival contributes to UNESCO's and the United Nations' goals of promoting peace and sustainable development by 2030.

The festival features cultural and traditional heritage summits, workshops, and captivating performances by global cultural groups. Participants will engage in activities that promote cultural diplomacy, business development, and heritage preservation.

Mission & Vision

Mission: Bridging global Afro-Diaspora cultural gaps to foster diversity, inclusion, and socioeconomic development while promoting peaceful coexistence in a multicultural environment.

Vision: Preserving and promoting global diaspora cultural excellence, creating platforms to enhance peace and development initiatives worldwide, and advocating for universal prosperity.

Goals

  • Promote peace and cultural unity through education and cultural diplomacy.
  • Encourage socioeconomic development and cultural entrepreneurship.
  • Preserve tangible and intangible cultural heritage globally.

Festival Activities

  • Welcome and arrival ceremonies for global participants.
  • Cultural and traditional summits featuring global royalty and heritage displays.
  • Business and cultural workshops in schools and community centers.
  • Global Poetry Festival and cultural games.
  • Musical performances, traditional dances, and arts exhibitions.

How You Can Help

Join us in celebrating and preserving cultural heritage through the Broken Cultures Festival. Your contributions and participation make a difference in promoting peace and cultural unity.
